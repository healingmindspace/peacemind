# Science & Psychology Education — Know Your Brain

## Vision
Help people understand the science behind their emotions, behavior, and personality. Not textbook-level — accessible, visual, and relevant to daily life. "Oh, THAT's why I feel this way."

## Why This Matters
- Knowing serotonin is low feels less scary than "something is wrong with me"
- Understanding your personality type reduces self-judgment
- Science removes stigma — depression is brain chemistry, not weakness
- Knowledge is empowering — you can work WITH your brain instead of against it

## Content Categories

### 🧠 How Your Brain Works
- **The emotional brain**: amygdala (fear/threat), prefrontal cortex (decisions), hippocampus (memory)
- **Fight-or-flight vs rest-and-digest**: sympathetic vs parasympathetic nervous system
- **Why anxiety feels physical**: the brain-body connection, vagus nerve
- **Neuroplasticity**: your brain can change — habits literally rewire neural pathways
- **The gut-brain axis**: 90% of serotonin made in gut, why diet affects mood
- **Sleep and the brain**: memory consolidation, emotional processing, glymphatic system cleanup

### 💊 Brain Chemistry (Neurotransmitters)
- **Serotonin**: mood, sleep, appetite — what happens when it's low, how to support it naturally (sunlight, exercise, tryptophan)
- **Dopamine**: motivation, reward, pleasure — why doom-scrolling hijacks it, healthy dopamine habits
- **Norepinephrine**: alertness, focus, stress response — too much = anxiety, too little = fatigue
- **GABA**: the calm chemical — what it does, how breathing exercises boost it
- **Cortisol**: stress hormone — acute vs chronic, why chronic stress is toxic, how to lower it
- **Oxytocin**: connection, trust, bonding — why hugs and social connection heal
- **Endorphins**: natural painkillers — exercise, laughter, music

### 🎭 Personality & Temperament
- **The Big Five (OCEAN)**: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism — what they mean, not good/bad
- **Introvert vs Extrovert**: it's about energy, not shyness — how to honor your wiring
- **Attachment styles**: secure, anxious, avoidant, disorganized — how childhood shapes adult relationships
- **Temperament**: what you're born with vs what you learn — nature AND nurture
- **Highly Sensitive Person (HSP)**: not a disorder, a trait — 20% of people, deeper processing
- **Self-assessment**: where do you fall? (interactive, not diagnostic)

### 🔄 Why We Do What We Do
- **Cognitive biases**: negativity bias (why bad news sticks), confirmation bias, catastrophizing
- **Habits loop**: cue → routine → reward — how to build and break habits
- **Emotional regulation**: window of tolerance, why you shut down or explode
- **Trauma responses**: fight, flight, freeze, fawn — recognizing them in yourself
- **Procrastination**: it's emotional regulation, not laziness — the science behind it
- **Social comparison**: why Instagram makes you sad — social brain meets digital world

### 🌱 Growth & Resilience
- **Post-traumatic growth**: some people grow stronger after hardship — what makes the difference
- **Learned helplessness vs learned optimism**: Seligman's research
- **Flow state**: when challenge matches skill — how to find it
- **Meaning and purpose**: Viktor Frankl, why having a "why" matters for mental health
- **Gratitude science**: how gratitude physically changes brain structure over time

## Format in App

### In Heal (Calm → Understand)
- Add topics as expandable cards (like existing anxiety/depression)
- Short, digestible — each topic fits in 2-3 paragraphs
- Bilingual EN/ZH
- Link to related app features: "Try the breathing exercise to activate your parasympathetic system"

### In Community (future)
- Expert-written deep dives
- Weekly "Science of..." posts
- Interactive: "Did you know?" in daily comfort messages

### In Move (future)
- Exercise + brain chemistry: "This walk just boosted your serotonin and BDNF"
- Post-workout science: "Here's what just happened in your brain"

## Implementation Plan

- "Did you know?" cards in daily comfort / Heal Insight

### Phase 3: Science-Informed AI
- Healer references science in responses: "That's your amygdala doing its job — it's trying to protect you"
- AI insights include brain chemistry context: "Exercise days correlate with better mood — that's the serotonin and endorphin boost"

## Content Principles
- **Accessible**: no jargon without explanation
- **Empowering**: knowledge that helps you act, not just understand
- **Non-judgmental**: personality types aren't good or bad, just different
- **Accurate**: reviewed by experts, cited where possible
- **Connected**: every science piece links to something you can do in the app
